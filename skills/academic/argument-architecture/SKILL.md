# Argument Architecture Skill

Strategic positioning (CARS) + Argumentative persuasion (Toulmin) = Publishable scholarship.

## Two-Level Architecture

| Level | Model | Function | Unit |
|-------|-------|----------|------|
| **Macro** | CARS | Position paper in scholarly conversation | Sections, especially Introduction |
| **Micro** | Toulmin | Build persuasive individual arguments | Sentences, paragraphs |

**CARS** answers: "Why should anyone read this paper?"  
**Toulmin** answers: "Why should anyone believe this claim?"

## Swales' CARS Model (Macro)

**C**reate **A** **R**esearch **S**pace — The Introduction as strategic argument.

### Move 1: Establish Territory

Convince readers the topic matters.

| Step | Function | Signal Phrases |
|------|----------|----------------|
| 1a | Claim centrality | "X is a fundamental concern...", "X plays a crucial role..." |
| 1b | Topic generalizations | "X typically involves...", "Research on X has shown..." |
| 1c | Review prior research | "Previous work establishes...", "Scholars have demonstrated..." |

**Purpose**: Reader concludes "Yes, this is important territory."

### Move 2: Establish Niche (The Gap)

Create the space your paper fills. This is the critical move.

| Step | Function | Signal Phrases |
|------|----------|----------------|
| 2a | Counter-claim | "However, this approach fails to...", "Yet these accounts overlook..." |
| 2b | Indicate gap | "What remains unclear is...", "Less attention has been paid to..." |
| 2c | Raise questions | "This raises the question...", "It remains to be determined..." |
| 2d | Continue tradition | "Following X's call for...", "Extending this line of inquiry..." |

**Purpose**: Reader concludes "Something is missing here."

### Move 3: Occupy Niche (Your Contribution)

Present what you offer.

| Step | Function | Signal Phrases |
|------|----------|----------------|
| 3a | Outline purpose | "This paper argues that...", "We propose..." |
| 3b | Announce findings | "The analysis reveals...", "We demonstrate..." |
| 3c | Indicate structure | "The paper proceeds as follows...", "Section 2 develops..." |

**Purpose**: Reader concludes "This paper fills the gap."

For extended guidance: `references/cars-model.md`

## Toulmin Model (Micro)

Six components of every argument:

| Component | Question | Role |
|-----------|----------|------|
| **Claim** | What are you asserting? | The conclusion you want accepted |
| **Data** | What's your evidence? | Facts, citations, observations |
| **Warrant** | Why does data support claim? | The logical bridge (often implicit) |
| **Backing** | What supports the warrant? | Theory, methodology, authority |
| **Qualifier** | How certain? | Scope conditions, hedges |
| **Rebuttal** | What are the limits? | Exceptions, counterarguments |

### Minimum Viable Argument

Every academic claim needs:
1. **Claim** — explicit assertion
2. **Data** — cited evidence
3. **Warrant** — defensible connection (can be implicit if obvious)

### Argument Diagnosis

| Symptom | Diagnosis | Treatment |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| "So what?" | Missing warrant | Explain why evidence matters |
| "Says who?" | Missing data | Add citations |
| "Always?" | Missing qualifier | Add scope conditions |
| "But what about...?" | Missing rebuttal | Address objection |
| "Why should I believe that?" | Missing backing | Ground the warrant |

For extended guidance: `references/toulmin-model.md`

## Integration: CARS → Toulmin

Each CARS move contains Toulmin arguments:

### Move 1 as Argument
- **Claim**: This topic matters
- **Data**: Prior research, real-world significance
- **Warrant**: Scholarly attention indicates importance

### Move 2 as Argument  
- **Claim**: A gap exists
- **Data**: What's missing in existing work
- **Warrant**: This gap matters because...
- **Rebuttal**: Acknowledge what existing work does accomplish

### Move 3 as Argument
- **Claim**: This paper fills the gap
- **Data**: Preview of findings/framework
- **Warrant**: Our approach succeeds where others failed
- **Qualifier**: Scope and limitations

## The "So What?" Test

Apply at three levels:

| Level | Question | If Weak |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Paper | Why does this contribution matter? | Sharpen Move 2 gap |
| Section | Why is this section here? | Connect to thesis |
| Paragraph | Why does this claim matter? | Add warrant |

## Contribution Types

Not all gaps are equal. Strong contributions:

| Type | What You Do | Signal |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **Theoretical extension** | Add to existing framework | "We extend X by..." |
| **Theoretical synthesis** | Integrate separate streams | "Bringing together X and Y reveals..." |
| **Boundary conditions** | Show where theory breaks | "X holds only when..." |
| **Conceptual clarification** | Resolve ambiguity | "We distinguish between..." |
| **New mechanism** | Explain how/why | "The mechanism connecting X to Y..." |
| **Problematization** | Challenge assumptions | "Contrary to received wisdom..." |

## Quick Diagnostics

### Introduction Weak?
- [ ] Move 1 present? (Territory established?)
- [ ] Move 2 sharp? (Gap clear and consequential?)
- [ ] Move 3 responsive? (Contribution addresses the gap?)
- [ ] Moves in sequence? (Territory → Gap → Contribution?)

### Argument Weak?
- [ ] Claim explicit?
- [ ] Data present and cited?
- [ ] Warrant defensible?
- [ ] Qualifier appropriate?
- [ ] Obvious rebuttals addressed?

### "So What?" Failing?
- [ ] Gap is trivial → Find a more consequential gap
- [ ] Warrant missing → Explain why evidence matters
- [ ] No stakes → Connect to broader concerns

## Skill Hierarchy: When to Use What

When writing academic texts, apply skills hierarchically:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  CARS (Macro)                                           │
│  → Governs SECTION logic                                │
│  → Positions paper in literature                        │
│  → Structures Introduction, frames entire argument      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  TOULMIN (Meso)                                         │
│  → Governs ARGUMENT logic                               │
│  → Structures claims within Theory & Discussion         │
│  → Ensures each argument is complete and defensible     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  ACADEMIC-WRITING / PEEL (Micro)                        │
│  → Governs PHRASING                                     │
│  → Realizes already-defined arguments in prose          │
│  → Handles style, citation integration, paragraph flow  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Decision Rules

| Question | Skill |
|----------|-------|
| "Where does this section fit in the paper?" | CARS |
| "Is this argument complete and persuasive?" | Toulmin |
| "Is this paragraph well-written?" | Academic-Writing |

### Workflow Sequence

1. **Plan with CARS**: What territory? What gap? What contribution?
2. **Structure with Toulmin**: What claims? What data? What warrants?
3. **Write with PEEL**: Point, Evidence, Explanation, Link

**Critical**: PEEL realizes arguments—it does not invent or justify them. If a paragraph feels weak, diagnose with Toulmin first (missing warrant? missing data?), then fix the prose with PEEL.

### Integration Example

**CARS decides**: "Section 3 occupies the niche by presenting my theoretical framework."

**Toulmin structures**: "The section's main claim is X, supported by data Y, warranted by Z, qualified by W."

**PEEL executes**: "Paragraph 1 states the claim (Point), presents evidence (Evidence), explains significance (Explanation), transitions to next argument (Link)."

## Mandatory Quality Layer: Anti-AI Writing

**This skill always activates the `anti-ai-writing` skill as a non-negotiable filter.**

Argument architecture and AI-pattern avoidance are not separate concerns — they are the same concern at different levels of resolution. The standard AI tells (present-participle inflation, comprehensiveness-by-triplet, transition-word synthesis) are structural symptoms of arguments that have not fully committed to a position. When fixing surface-level AI patterns, check whether the underlying argumentative move is also incomplete.

Apply standard prohibitions + Academic Module on all outputs. Pay specific attention to:
- **Gap statements** that describe absence vaguely ("little attention has been paid to...") rather than specifying what is missing and why it matters
- **Contribution statements** hedged into near-invisibility ("this paper aims to contribute to...")
- **CARS moves** that acknowledge existing work without taking a clear position on it
